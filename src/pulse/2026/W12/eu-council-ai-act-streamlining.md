## What happened

The EU Council agreed its position on March 13, 2026 to streamline certain rules in the EU AI Act as part of the "Omnibus VII" simplification package. The proposal adjusts the timeline for applying high-risk AI system rules by up to 16 months, deferring enforcement until the European Commission confirms that the necessary standards and tools are available. It also extends regulatory exemptions previously granted to SMEs to include small mid-caps, and reinforces the AI Office's powers while reducing governance fragmentation.

## Why it matters

This signals that even the EU — which led the world in AI regulation — recognizes the practical difficulty of enforcing rules before standards are ready. The 16-month delay gives companies more time to prepare, and extending SME exemptions to small mid-caps broadens the pool of companies with lighter compliance obligations. For teams building AI products for the European market, this provides breathing room but does not remove the obligation to comply.

## Who should pay attention

- Companies selling AI products or services in the EU market
- Compliance and legal teams tracking AI Act implementation timelines
- Small and mid-sized AI companies that may benefit from extended exemptions
