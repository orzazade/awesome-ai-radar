## What happened

OpenAI reduced GPT-5 Turbo API pricing by 40%, bringing input tokens to $1.50/M and output tokens to $6/M. The cut follows Google's Gemini 2.5 Pro pricing announcement last month and Anthropic's volume discount program. OpenAI cited inference optimization improvements and competitive pressure as drivers.

## Why it matters

This is the continuation of a predictable trend: frontier model pricing drops every few months as competition heats up and inference infrastructure improves. The 40% cut is meaningful for high-volume API consumers but doesn't change the strategic calculus for most teams. If you were already building on GPT-5, your costs just went down. If you weren't, this alone isn't a reason to switch. The real story is that frontier model inference is commoditizing faster than anyone expected — the differentiation is shifting to tooling, ecosystem, and specialized capabilities.

## Who should pay attention

Engineering managers budgeting for AI API costs, teams running high-volume inference workloads on OpenAI, and CFOs who need updated cost projections for AI initiatives.
